# Six-point checklist — before a characteristic is activated

A candidate characteristic (a proposed trait to measure and track) does not enter the active
catalog until it has an answer to all six of the following. Missing any one of them means the
characteristic stays `dormant — no source`, the same status a characteristic gets when the data
required to measure it doesn't exist yet.

1. **What exactly is counted** — the indicator itself, stated precisely enough that two people
   measuring it would agree on the number.
2. **How it's measured** — the method: which signal, which formula, which data source.
3. **Convergent validity** — does it agree with independent signs of the same underlying trait,
   not just with itself measured a different way?
4. **Discriminant validity** — is it actually different from a characteristic already in the
   catalog under another name? Guards against silently duplicating a trait.
5. **Resistance to gaming** (Goodhart's law) — once this becomes a target, can someone move the
   number without moving the underlying trait? A metric that fails this stops being useful the
   moment it starts being watched.
6. **Current evidence rung** — per the rung discipline in `discipline.md`: is this characteristic
   currently a rung-1 hypothesis, or has it earned rung-2 through an actual intervention?

No shortcuts: a characteristic that scores well on five of six stays dormant until the sixth is
answered too.
