# Discipline

Three rules govern every claim this lab makes. Skipping any one of them turns a real finding
into a guess wearing the clothes of a finding.

## 1. Pre-registration before analysis

The hypothesis, the metric that decides it, the sample, and the period are written down and
committed *before* the first query touches the data. A hypothesis edited after seeing the
result is not being tested — it is being fitted. See `pre-registration-template.md` for the
format this lab uses.

## 2. Rung discipline — every finding states its evidence level

- **Rung 1** — an observed correlation. It says two things moved together, nothing about why.
- **Rung 2** — a causal claim, earned only through an intervention (a deliberate change,
  observed effect, on a real cohort) — never through a bigger pile of correlational data.

A report that omits the rung is making a stronger claim than it can support. Rung-collapse —
writing rung-1 findings in language that implies rung-2 — is the single most common way
observational research misleads its readers, and it is explicitly forbidden here.

## 3. Use-collapse is forbidden

A calibration result — even a solid, rung-1, well-replicated one — does not automatically
become a recommendation. "This correlates with progress" and "therefore do this" are different
claims requiring different evidence; only the second requires an intervention trial. This lab
publishes the first kind and marks clearly when the second kind has not been earned yet.

## Where this comes from

This lab's discipline follows FPF C.28 (the first-principles account of pre-registration and
rung tracking) as applied inside the IWE research method FORM.093 §6.4. Both sources are
private — this page is the public distillation, not a mirror.
